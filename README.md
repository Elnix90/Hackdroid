# HackDroid ![Hackdroid icon](https://github.com/Elnix90/Hackdroid/blob/c8b435a60ef9f2b30597671d6b1cc58e2660bdd3/site/src/jsMain/resources/public/ugly-fdroid.png)

Build a useful Android app and deploy it on F-Droid to get rewards!

Hackdroid helps students create FOSS Android apps and share them on F-Droid to grow the open-source community.

## The Process

Students will:
* Build an Android app using Kotlin, or whatever they want to work with
* Open their MR to F-Droid's GitLab, following their contribution guidelines
* After they get accepted, or rejected, they will unlock the rewards

## What Students Learn

* Android development, using Kotlin / Jetpack Compose
* Build a concrete idea, as well as having the right project structure, to stay in F-Droid's requirements
* How to package their finished app (.apk or .aab)
* The submission process to FOSS software, publishing it to F-Droid

## Rewards

### If successfully published to F-Droid
* **$50 Phone Grant** - Cumulative, to buy a phone (20 hours)

### If not accepted by F-Droid
* **$25 Google Play License** (10 hours) - Allow them to publish their app on Play Store. This is less ideal for FOSS software, but they can still promote their app and get visibility
* **$10 VPS Grant** (5 hours) - Cumulative for backend servers (optional, some apps may need this)

## Time Tracking

We use two different systems:

### 1. Hakatime + Logs
* Track actual coding time using Hakatime
* System of multiple logs to force accurate progression tracking
* Students explain their progress through the creation process
* Logs must stay under 5 hours to prevent cheating and keep them focused on small ideas
* We don't expect them to build Google—just small apps like note-taking or calendar apps to help them discover Android dev

### 2. F-Droid Submission
* Students share their MR on GitLab
* We check if their app was successfully merged
* If accepted, they unlock the $50 phone grant
* They must work at least 20 hours per grant

## Marketing Strategy

* Send out announcements in HC's Slack to get people excited
* Reach out to people running other mobile-focused or app-based YSWS's to position HackDroid as the next step after completing a smaller project
* Use social media, such as my own Discord server to send out announcements (members are there to discuss my Android app, so they might be interested in this YSWS)
* Encourage people to share their app with school friends, to invite them to sign up for Hack Club and the YSWS
